]]> What is Etihad’s ‘Verified to Fly’?

Etihad has a program ‘Verified to Fly’ which is nothing but you can check whether you can be able to fly to your destination during COVID-19 pandemic. Passenger needs to send all the relevant documents regarding COVID-19.

Where is Etihad’s ‘Verified to Fly’ available?

Etihad’s ‘Verified to Fly’ is available at Abu Dhabi and all the worldwide airports except Bangladesh, China , India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka.

How and when to use Etihad’s ‘Verified to Fly’?

  1. 7 days before your flight you can use Etihad’s ‘Verified to Fly’.
  2. Passengers are required to send the COVID-19 PCR test result as soon as they received

How and where to send COVID-19 documents in Etihad Airways?

  1. Go to Manage my booking
  2. Enter email id and the contact details.
  3. Enter the booking reference / ticket number and passenger’s last name
  4. Once entered all the required details, you will receive an email regarding the required documents to attach depends upon your transit / final destination.
  5. Send the relevant documents through the email you have received.
  6. Submit the negative COVID-19 PCR test certificate.
  7. You can download the boarding pass also.
  8. Take that to the verified to fly desks at the airport.

How to check Etihad’s Verified to Fly status?

  1. Go to Manage my booking
  2. Enter email id and the contact details.
  3. Enter the booking reference / ticket number and passenger’s last name
  4. Check the status.
  5. In case if the flight is delayed or been cancelled, you need to re-submit the documents for ‘verified to fly’

Note: Verified to Fly service is optional to use

For more details, visit https://www.etihad.com/en/manage/verified-to-fly

]]> What are the Screening procedures for transgender persons at the airport

TSA makes sure all the travelers are treated equally with respect and courtesy where the screening procedure will be conducted without regard to a person’s race, color, sex, gender identity, national origin, religion or disability.

  1. Up on arrival to the airport security checkpoint, all the transgender persons must be screened at the security checkpoint using the advanced imaging technology.
  2. The advanced imaging technology use the software that looks the anatomy of men and women differently.
  3. If there is any alarm while screening, TSA officers will clear the alarm.
  4. If you need a private screening, you may request or to speak with the officer.

]]> What is Air Suvidha?

Air Suvidha is a contact-less and convenient online portal developed for international passengers traveling to India where the passengers must fill the Self Declaration / Reporting form and the Exemption form if applicable.

How to apply and obtain green signal in Air Suvidha and avoid red signal?

How to fill Air Suvidha Self Declaration / Self Reporting Form?

Note: All the international passengers must have RT-PCR negative certificate to upload to fill the Self Declaration Form / Self Reporting Form. Take the COVID-19 RT-PCR test 72 hours prior to the departure.

1. Go to https://www.newdelhiairport.in/airsuvidha/apho-registration

2. If you have exemption request number received from the filled exemption form, enter the request number.

3. Enter the contact phone number, passport number, flight number, PNR number.

4. Once entered all the above details, click next to proceed.

5. In the next screen you will be asked to fill name, date of birth, gender, flight number, seat number, nationality, Date of arrival, country of origin and departure, first airport of entry, your destination residential details (full address, pin code)

6. If you have any connecting domestic flight, declare it

7. If you have any symptoms such as Fever / cough / cold: declare it

8. If you are suffering from Diabetes / Hypertension / Bronchial Asthma / Under Immunosuppresive Therapy / Post Transplant Patients: declare it

9. Enter the last visited cities / countries in the last 14 days

10. Enter the number of passengers traveling

11. You need to upload your passport copy, RT-PCR negative test certificate

12. Once declared all the details, agree for the terms and conditions and click ‘Submit’.

13. Once submitted, the details will be sent to the health authority for the verification.

14. Finally, an email will be received for the confirmation.

How to fill Air Suvidha Exemption form? What are the documents required to submit for the exemption category?

Who is in exempted category in Air Suvidha?

Apply for Air Suvidha Exemption

For the passengers who fit into an Air Suvidha exemption category such as Pregnant, COVID-19 negative certificate, death in the family, a parent traveling with kids below 10 years old must

  1. Fill the required form
  2. Select the applicable exemption category
  3. Self reporting forms will be sent to APHO under MOHF
  4. If not, undergo institutional quarantine

1. Go to Exemption Request Form For International Arriving Passengers 

2. If you have Self request number received from the filled self declaration form, enter the request number.

3. Enter the contact phone number, passport number, flight number, PNR number.

4. Once entered all the above details, click next to proceed.

5. In the next screen you will be asked to fill name, date of birth, gender, flight number, seat number, nationality, Date of arrival, country of origin and departure, first airport of entry, your destination residential details (full address, pin code).

6. Enter the number of passengers traveling

7. You need to upload your passport copy,  Supporting documents if any.

8. Passengers must undergo institutional quarantine (self paid) if they do not have valid RT-PCR test result.

Passengers can apply online to seek exemption from mandatory Institution Quarantine under five categories

Documents required to submit for the Air Suvidha exemption form:

Passengers fall on the exempted category must provide the supporting documents. Please check the below list:

  • Pregnant woman must provide the valid recommendation letter from the doctor.
  • Parent’s accompanied by children under 10 years old must provide the passport copy
  • Serious illness if any: must attach the medical certificate
  • Death if any: Attach death/doctor certificate; Family as defined by the Indian Government consists of Spouse, Children, Sibling, Parents & Grandparents only
  • Attach RT-PCR negative report or a copy of RT-PCR tests where results are awaited

*Once submitted all the details and the necessary documents, you will obtain the green signal in Air Suvidha. If the passenger fails to upload any document or RT-PCR test negative certificate, they may obtain a red signal in Air Suvidha

How to track Air Suvidha application status?

To check the status of the Air Suvidha application, click here

  • Enter the request number (Self declaration request number / exemption request number).
  • Enter Passport number
  • Enter the date of birth
  • Submit the details and find out the status.

Book COVID RT-PCR test

Passengers can book COVID-19 test through the above link

MoHFW’s 24 hours helpline number 011-23978406.

]]> How to apply and obtain Air Travel Pass (ATP) or ATP approval to visit or travel to Singapore?

With effect from 31 January 2021, 11.59pm, Passengers who are traveling to Singapore under ATP pass or ATP approval must have travel insurance for COVID-19-related medical treatment with coverage of S$30,000.

Who are eligible to apply for ATP Pass or ATP approval?

  1. Passengers who comes in short-visit visa to Singapore from the below listed location are required to apply for Air Travel Pass (ATP) or ATP approval
    • Australia (excluding NSW),
    • Brunei Darussalam,
    • China,
    • New Zealand,
    • Thailand,
    • Vietnam
  2. Singapore Citizens, Permanent Residents and holders of Long Term Passes (such as Long-Term Visit Pass, Student Pass, Work Pass and Dependant’s Pass) are not required to apply for Air Travel Pass (ATP) or ATP approval.

Procedure to apply Air Travel Pass (ATP) or ATP approval to Singapore

Applicant must submit the application between 7 to 30 days prior to the departure.

  1. Apply for ATP pass or ATP approval through https://capels.caas.gov.sg/atp
  2. You need to enter the departure status. From which country you are traveling to Singapore.
  3. Enter the flight details such as flight number and personal details such as name, date of birth, mobile number, email. Once verified your email, you will receive OTP to your email.
  4. Enter the nationality, passport number, passport expiry date.
  5. Enter the travel history
  6. Finally apply to get the approval. You will receive ATP reference number.
  7. ATP approval will take 3 working days to be processed. Once you get the approval, you will be subject to COVID-19 test upon arrival to Singapore and period of SHN (Stay home notice).
  8. Passengers traveling from the above mentioned countries can pre-pay for the post COVID-19 PCR test at https://safetravel.changiairport.com. COVID-19 PCR test cost at Changi airport, Singapore is S$149.53 (S$160 GST inclusive)
  9. For further queries on applying ATP, passengers can email to COVID_ATP_Enquiries@caas.gov.sg.

Check Singapore ATP Status here

By entering ATP reference number, you can find the air travel pass (ATP) status Singapore.

Required documents to travel Singapore

Passengers must show the below mentioned documents upon arrival or at the departure in Singapore.

  • Approved ATP pass letter (printout or soft copy).
  • a valid visa (for visa-required passport holders); and
  • Downloaded and registered TraceTogether app in a mobile device.

Terms and conditions to be followed for ATP approval / ATP pass

  1. The above mentioned countries who are visiting under ATP approval must come in direct flight to Singapore without transit.
  2. Passengers must have stayed for consecutive 14 days at the above mentioned countries.
  3. Passengers will be ready for post COVID-19 PCR test upon arrival in Singapore airport where the test result will be obtained in 12 hours to 48 hours.
  4. Once the PCR test has given, passengers must hire a private transportation to stay at non-residential individual room with attached toilet.
  5. Visit here for Singapore Hotel Association (SHA) or you can book other available option.
  6. Download and register at TraceTogether app  where you need to upload the data for 14 days.
  7. Passengers are responsible for COVID-19 medical bills while they are in Singapore.

]]> Generally IATA Codes are used in the field of airline and travel industry which is very important for the purpose of an airline identification, passenger and cargo traffic documents. The travel agents who wants to sell the international or domestic tickets are required to obtain IATA number

IATA Codes are classified into

  • Airline Designator Codes
  • Accounting or Prefix codes
  • Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC)
  • Location Identifier code

How to obtain TIDS (Travel Industry Designator Service) code?

What is TIDS code?

For the travel related business, one must obtain IATA/TIDS numeric code where TIDS is an IATA program having unique numeric code recognized by the travel industry suppliers globally which allows the travel agents to book the air travel tickets, hotel packages, car booking, rental/transfer, theme parks, cruises and any other tourism related sales.

*The IATA TIDS Program is available globally except in the USA.

Benefits of having TIDS code

  • TIDS code is easily identified by the travel industry suppliers globally.
  • Airline ticket booking/packages, Hotel booking and Car Rental booking can be easily done using TIDS code.
  • TIDS code is used in the sales of non-accredited points of sale.
  • Some suppliers also require an IATA/TIDS Code for onboarding on their distribution platforms.

*TIDS agent will not be able to issue airline tickets through IATA’s BSP (Billing and Settlement Plan)

Eligibility to apply for TIDS code

Person who are in the travel related / tourism related business such as air travel, car rental, hotel booking, theme parks, cruise booking are eligible to apply for TIDS code.

Procedure to apply for TIDS code

  • If you are a new user, register through IATA Customer Portal
  • And if you are an existing user, log in through IATA Customer Portal
  • Click TIDS icon under ‘Services’ tab.
  • Submit the required documents mentioned below.
  • Complete & Submit your TIDS Application
  • Once the TIDS application form is submitted online along with the required documents, your application will be processed by IATA within 3 to 5 working days.

Documents required to apply for TIDS

  • Supporting documents related to the legal form of business and ownership of the business entity.
  • Business registration number / tax registration number copy
  • Letter from the bank or bank account statement in the business name.
  • One recommendation letter from an IATA Airline member, GDS (i.e. Amadeus, Sabre, Worldspan…) or any major travel industry suppliers (i.e. air travel, cruise, hotels, car rentals, theme parks)
  • License related to sales / travel services issued from Government (if applicable). For example, a copy of the provincial travel agency license issued by Canadian government located in QC (Quebec), ON (Ontario), and BC (British Colombia) must be submitted by an applicant. A copy of the license issued by Turkish Ministry of Tourism & Culture must be submitted by an applicant.
  • A signed copy of the  TIDS Terms and Conditions.

]]> The purpose of IATA Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC) is a unique number to identify the name of the passenger, flight details, and destination.

Procedures and documents required to obtain IATA Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC)

To obtain IATA Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC), the following documents are required:

  1. Submit the online application form which is available in www.iata.org/cs. The application form must be received from either an airline or from CRS
  2. Air Operator Certificate (AOC) issued by a government.
  3. Photocopies of company’s Certificate of Incorporation/Registration (COI/COR)
  4. Breakdown of company’s list of shareholders including percentage ownership of each shareholder.
  5. The document should be on corporate letterhead and dully signed and stamped with a corporate seal.
  6. Documents (no. 3 and 4) must be translated in English. It will be signed and certified by official translator.

IATA Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC) application and assignment cost

The cost of obtaining IATA Baggage Tag Issuer Code (BTIC) is USD 5,700.00 (as of 2020)
