Tiger Airways Australia Pty Ltd, operating as Tigerair Australia, is an Australian low-cost airline. It commenced services in the Australian domestic airline market on 23 November 2007 as Tiger Airways Australia. It is a fully owned subsidiary of Virgin Australia Holdings.[5] The airline is based in Melbourne, Victoria, with its main base at Melbourne Airport. The Civil Aviation Safety Authority of Australia (CASA) grounded the airline in 2011; as a result operating bases at Adelaide and Avalon were shut down and it initially only operated out of its Melbourne base after returning to the air. On 7 March 2012, Tigerair announced the reopening of a second base at Sydney Airport.

Travel during pregnancy in Tigerair

Single and Multiple Pregnancy for flights less than 4 hours in Tigerair

Expectant mothers can travel in Tigerair up to 40 weeks for single pregnancy and 36 weeks for multiple pregnancy.

For single pregnancy, no medical certificate is required for expectant mothers who travel from 0 to 27 weeks and a medical certificate is required who travel from 28 to 40 weeks.

For multiple pregnancy, no medical certificate is required for expectant mothers who travel from 0 to 27 weeks and a medical certificate is required who travel from 28 to 36 weeks.

Single and Multiple Pregnancy for flights more than 4 hours in Tigerair

Expectant mothers can travel in Tigerair up to 36 weeks for single pregnancy and 32 weeks for multiple pregnancy.

For single pregnancy, no medical certificate is required for expectant mothers who travel from 0 to 27 weeks and a medical certificate is required who travel from 28 to 36 weeks.

For multiple pregnancy, no medical certificate is required for expectant mothers who travel from 0 to 27 weeks and a medical certificate is required who travel from 28 to 32 weeks.
