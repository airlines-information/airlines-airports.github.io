What to expect for your travel?

Procedures to travel or enter into UAE

  1. Passenger must obtain ICA approval / GDRFA approval before the departure.(check below for how to obtain ICA / GDRFA approval below).
  2. Once approved, book your flight to UAE
  3. Passengers are required to have negative COVID‑19 RT PCR test certificate 72 hours prior to the departure.
  4. Non vaccinated passengers are required to subject under quarantine and required to repeat the COVID‑19 RT‑PCR test on 6th and 9th day
  5. Vaccinated passengers are not required to subject under quarantine and required to repeat the COVID‑19 RT‑PCR test on 6th day.
  6. COVID‑19 RT PCR test result must be accepted from the UAE recognized lab. Download the QR code to show at the check-in
  7. The following countries have to take COVID‑19 RT PCR test on arrival at the airport in UAE
    • Afghanistan, Angola, Argentina, Bahrain, Bangladesh, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Brazil, Cambodia, Chile, Croatia, Cyprus, Djibouti, Egypt, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Georgia, Ghana, Guinea, Hungary, India, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Ivory Coast, Jordan, Kenya, Kuwait, Kyrgyzstan, Lebanon, Malta, Moldova, Montenegro, Morocco, Myanmar, Nepal, Oman, Pakistan, Poland, Philippines, Qatar, Rwanda, Russia, Senegal, Slovakia, Somaliland, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Syria, Tajikistan, Tanzania, Tunisia, Turkey, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, Uzbekistan, Zimbabwe.
  8. Passengers traveling to Abu Dhabi must download Al Hosn app (iOS‑Android and update their COVID status

Type of COVID-19 test accepted: COVID RT-PCR test

Documents required to enter UAE

  • ICA/GDRFA approval
  • COVID-19 Negative test report
  • Passport
  • Visa (if required)

Traveling to Hong Kong during COVID 19

During this COVID 19 pandemic situation, passengers who wants to travel or transit to Hong Kong must follow the below mentioned procedures

Note: Before plan to travel to Hong Kong, please check with your travel agent or airline about the further requirements.

  1. Passengers must use online form for the health declaration (Check the details below)
  2. Effective from 1st March 2021, government employees and members of the public must scan the QR code from the LeaveHomeSafe mobile app
  3. Passengers must take RT-PCR nucleic acid test and provide a negative certificate
  4. Book the designated hotels for quarantine. (Check the list below)
  5. Once arrived into the airport, you need to take COVID test (nasal and throat swab).
  6. If the test result is negative, you can proceed to the quarantine place and if the test result is positive, you will be guided by the health professionals.
  7. Once you arrived into quarantine location, activate StayHomeSafe app (follow the instruction)
  8. You are required to take test on 11th day by the government provided kit.
  9. On the 19th or 20th day following your arrival into Hong Kong, you will undergo another test in accordance with the instructions provided

Download StayHomeSafe app for android users and apple users

COVID test type accepted to enter in to Hong Kong

COVID-19 test type: RT-PCR nucleic acid test

How to use health declaration form to travel Hong Kong?

Submit the health declaration form to HKSAR Department of Health: Health & Quarantine Information Declaration for the travel.

  1. Go to the the online health declaration form
  2. Select the port you want to enter
  3. Enter the personal details (Name, gender, date of birth, nationality, ID card number,  Passport number etc.)
  4. Enter the recent travel history
  5. Enter the symptom of fever / cold / cough / sore throat / breathing difficulty if any
  6. Enter the flight details
  7. If you have completed a full cycle / course of COVID-19 vaccination 14 days before the scheduled departure date, mention as yes or no
  8. Enter the contact details and quarantine details.
  9. Once completed all the details, a QR code will be generated which you need to show on the arrival to the airport.
  10. QR code is valid for 48 hours

Traveling to Canada

During this COVID 19 pandemic situation, passengers who wants to travel or transit to Canada must follow the below mentioned procedures

Note: Before plan to travel to Canada, please check with your travel agent or airline about the further requirements.

  1. Use ArriveCAN to enter Canada (Download for Apple (IOS), Google play store / Android users)
  2. Effective from 23 rd April 2021, passengers who arrives Canada must take test on Day-8

How to use / apply ArriveCAN?

Passengers are required to submit the information mentioned below online prior to the departure or before arrival at a land border crossing

  • Sign in to ArriveCAN using your email id and create account.
  • You will receive a confirmation code in your email. Click the confirmation code with in 24 hours.
  • Enter the travel history
  • Enter the contact details
  • Quarantine plan (unless exempted from the mandatory requirement to quarantine set out in the emergency order under the Quarantine Act)
  • COVID-19 symptom self-assessment
  • government-authorized hotel details.
  • You can also scan your passport, FAST card, NEXUS card, enhanced driver’s licence for easy access to your personal and contact details.
  • Once completed the ArriveCAN application, you will receive a 6 digit code as a proof of submitting the application. Keep that as safe.

COVID-19 test type and requirements while traveling to Canada

Canadian government has released the latest COVID 19 test type to accept for the passengers who wants to enter Canada. Children above 5 years also included to take COVID 19 molecular tests

  • PCR – Polymerase chain reaction
  • RT-PCR – reverse transcription real time PCR
  • Quantitative PCR (qPCR)
  • Nucleic acid test (NAT) or Nucleic acid amplification test (NAATs)
  • Reverse transcription loop-mediated isothermal amplification (RT-LAMP)
  • Isothermal amplification
  • Droplet digital PCR or digital droplet PCR (ddPCR)
  • Transcription-mediated amplification (TMA)
  • RNA (Ribonucleic acid)
  • Ct (cycle threshold)
  • CRISPR
  • Sequencing
  • Next generational sequencing (NGS) or whole genome sequencing (WGS)
  • Oxford Nanopore sequencing (LamPORE)
  • Detection of the N gene
  • Detection of Orf1a/b
  • Detection of the S gene
  • Detection of the E gene
  • Detection of the RdRp gene

These tests use methods such as a nasopharyngeal (NP) swab, nose swab, or saliva sample. At this time, proof of having a vaccine will not replace a negative test result.

How COVID 19 test will be taken?

  • nasopharyngeal (NP) swab (Throat swab)
  • nose swab
  • saliva sample

Traveling to China during COVID-19

During this COVID 19 pandemic situation, passengers who wants to travel or transit to China must follow the below mentioned procedures

Note: Before plan to travel to China, please check with your travel agent or airline about the further requirements.

Passengers must take PCR + serological IgM antibody test (blood test) which costs around EUR 195.00 including VAT. Effective from November 2020, passengers arriving to China from COVID-19 (Corona virus) risk areas will be required to submit a valid negative COVID-19 certificate

  • If the passenger is fully vaccinated, they are required to submit a COVID-19 negative certificate and vaccine certificate. No need to self quarantine. Get viral test after arrival.
  • If the passenger is not vaccinated, they are required to submit a COVID-19 negative certificate and viral test prior 1 to 2 days before departure. They must be in self quarantine after arrival.

Procedure to travel back to China

  • Passengers must take the new coronavirus nucleic acid test at the recognized center 3 days prior to the departure.
  • Apply for a green health code with the “HS” mark or a health declaration code (QR code) with a negative test certificate.
  • You can apply for “HS/HDC” at the Chinese Embassy or consulate in your departing country. For this passengers must take direct flights from their departing country to China. Transit passenger will not able to apply for “HS/HDC” to travel to China.
  • For more details, visit your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate website

How to apply for HS/HDC to travel to China?

What is HS / HDC?

*Health code / Health Declaration Code is a QR code generated and sent by Chinese embassy or consulate.

Step by step procedure to apply HS / HDC

  1. Passengers arriving to China must upload negative COVID-19 test certificate (PCR + IgM), travel itinerary, residence certificates, visas and other documents as required by Chinese embassy or consulate in your departing country.
  2. If the test result is positive, passenger needs to provide lung CT or X-ray examination report produced by local recognized hospital, as well as two PCR negative test certificates (with at least 24 hours interval). Passenger must undergo self quarantine for 14 days.
  3. After producing COVID 19 negative test certificate with in 48 hours prior to departure, Chinese embassy or consulate will issue “HS/HDC”.
  4. Passengers must present the electronic or printed code, and follow the procedures for inspection by relevant airlines during boarding.

Travelers arriving from US, Jordan, Saudi Arabia to China:

  1. Passengers arriving from US to China must produce negative COVID-19 nucleic acid RT-PCR Test certificate.
  2. Passengers arriving from Jordan to China must produce negative nucleic acid and IgM anti-body tests certificate within 48 hours before boarding the flights and then apply for a green health code with the “HS/HDC” mark.
  3. Passengers arriving from Saudi Arabia to China must produce negative nucleic acid test result at least 14 days before boarding the flight and negative nucleic acid test, IgM anti-body test results within 48 hours before boarding the flight
  4. Submit Health declaration form
  5. You need to upload the COVID test certificate in the health declaration form and submit.
  6. Once submitted, it will be sent for approval to Chinese embassy in your destination. Chinese embassy will verify and send Electronic Health Declaration Form, which will be displayed in QR code with the “HDC” mark. Passengers must present the electronic or printed code, and follow the procedures for inspection by relevant airlines during boarding.
  7. For any enquiries, email chinaconsularjo@hotmail.com (Jordan residents) and website

]]> What is Air Suvidha?

Air Suvidha is a contact-less and convenient online portal developed for international passengers traveling to India where the passengers must fill the Self Declaration / Reporting form and the Exemption form if applicable.

How to apply and obtain green signal in Air Suvidha and avoid red signal?

How to fill Air Suvidha Self Declaration / Self Reporting Form?

Note: All the international passengers must have RT-PCR negative certificate to upload to fill the Self Declaration Form / Self Reporting Form. Take the COVID-19 RT-PCR test 72 hours prior to the departure.

1. Go to https://www.newdelhiairport.in/airsuvidha/apho-registration

2. If you have exemption request number received from the filled exemption form, enter the request number.

3. Enter the contact phone number, passport number, flight number, PNR number.

4. Once entered all the above details, click next to proceed.

5. In the next screen you will be asked to fill name, date of birth, gender, flight number, seat number, nationality, Date of arrival, country of origin and departure, first airport of entry, your destination residential details (full address, pin code)

6. If you have any connecting domestic flight, declare it

7. If you have any symptoms such as Fever / cough / cold: declare it

8. If you are suffering from Diabetes / Hypertension / Bronchial Asthma / Under Immunosuppresive Therapy / Post Transplant Patients: declare it

9. Enter the last visited cities / countries in the last 14 days

10. Enter the number of passengers traveling

11. You need to upload your passport copy, RT-PCR negative test certificate

12. Once declared all the details, agree for the terms and conditions and click ‘Submit’.

13. Once submitted, the details will be sent to the health authority for the verification.

14. Finally, an email will be received for the confirmation.

How to fill Air Suvidha Exemption form? What are the documents required to submit for the exemption category?

Who is in exempted category in Air Suvidha?

Apply for Air Suvidha Exemption

For the passengers who fit into an Air Suvidha exemption category such as Pregnant, COVID-19 negative certificate, death in the family, a parent traveling with kids below 10 years old must

  1. Fill the required form
  2. Select the applicable exemption category
  3. Self reporting forms will be sent to APHO under MOHF
  4. If not, undergo institutional quarantine

1. Go to Exemption Request Form For International Arriving Passengers 

2. If you have Self request number received from the filled self declaration form, enter the request number.

3. Enter the contact phone number, passport number, flight number, PNR number.

4. Once entered all the above details, click next to proceed.

5. In the next screen you will be asked to fill name, date of birth, gender, flight number, seat number, nationality, Date of arrival, country of origin and departure, first airport of entry, your destination residential details (full address, pin code).

6. Enter the number of passengers traveling

7. You need to upload your passport copy,  Supporting documents if any.

8. Passengers must undergo institutional quarantine (self paid) if they do not have valid RT-PCR test result.

Passengers can apply online to seek exemption from mandatory Institution Quarantine under five categories

Documents required to submit for the Air Suvidha exemption form:

Passengers fall on the exempted category must provide the supporting documents. Please check the below list:

  • Pregnant woman must provide the valid recommendation letter from the doctor.
  • Parent’s accompanied by children under 10 years old must provide the passport copy
  • Serious illness if any: must attach the medical certificate
  • Death if any: Attach death/doctor certificate; Family as defined by the Indian Government consists of Spouse, Children, Sibling, Parents & Grandparents only
  • Attach RT-PCR negative report or a copy of RT-PCR tests where results are awaited

*Once submitted all the details and the necessary documents, you will obtain the green signal in Air Suvidha. If the passenger fails to upload any document or RT-PCR test negative certificate, they may obtain a red signal in Air Suvidha

How to track Air Suvidha application status?

To check the status of the Air Suvidha application, click here

  • Enter the request number (Self declaration request number / exemption request number).
  • Enter Passport number
  • Enter the date of birth
  • Submit the details and find out the status.

Passengers who are traveling to France must take a COVID‑19 RT‑PCR test (and not an antigenic) less than 72 hours before departure to France and subject to mandatory quarantine for 7 days after arrival. If the passenger has no valid test result, they cannot board the flight. Read the conditions mentioned below:

Travel to and from France is restricted except for nationals of European Union member states, Andorra, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Monaco, Norway, Saint‑Marin, La Switzerland and the Vatican. Passengers must present supporting documents for compelling professional, personal and emergency reasons to travel to be accepted on flights.

Type of COVID-19 test requirements while traveling to Austria. Procedure to take the COVID test

All the passengers other than from the below mentioned countries who are traveling to Austria must take a COVID-19 test – molecular‑biological/antigen‑Test before departure or after arrival to Austria and subject to mandatory quarantine after arrival. If the passenger has no valid test result, they cannot board the flight. Read the conditions mentioned below:

Passengers from the below countries have been or stayed for at least 10 days are not required to submit the COVID test and no mandatory quarantine .

  • Australia, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Japan, New Zealand, Norway, Republic Korea, Uruguay, Vatican.
  • Third country nationals arriving from out of Schengen or from out of countries not listed above could not enter Austria.

Pre‑travel clearance (PTC)Forms required to submit to enter Austria

  • Passenger entering Austria must register and obtain a pre‑travel clearance (PTC).
  • They must show the soft copy in their mobile or hard copy (print out).
  • Enter the personal details such as name, contact number, email
  • Enter the address details such as residence address, etc
  • Enter the travel details such as country of departure, mode of transport, vehicle number or flight number etc.

Apply and obtain Pre-Travel Clearance (PTC) form Austria

  • PTC English version
  • PTC German version
  • Health Certificate when arriving from non exempted countries: https://tinyurl.com/medcerten201219
  • Confirmation of Medical Service in case of absolute medical necessity: https://tinyurl.com/medservice201017
  • Visit for more details, https://www.oesterreich.gv.at/en/themen/coronavirus_in_oesterreich/pre-travel-clearance.html

Travel or transit to Germany during COVID 19 situation: Procedures / Documents required

Mostly RT-PCR test will be conducted for the passengers arriving at the German airports. Effective from 11 th January 2021, passengers arriving to Germany from COVID-19 (Corona virus) risk areas will be required to submit a

  • a negative Covid-19 test certificate or
  • Completed COVID-19 Vaccination certificate
  • a medical certificate stated that the passenger has previously been infected with the COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2 ) at least 21 days and not more than six months ago (proof of immunity)

Quarantine at Germany after reaching Germany

There will be quarantine for 10 days. You can enquire your federal state for more details. To the quarantine regulations of the federal states (German only)

Digital Entry Application (DEA)

Passengers arriving to Germany requires to complete the Digital Entry Application (DEA) or a substitute application prior to departure

For whom this Digital Entry Application (DEA) is applicable, please read here

Procedures to fill Digital Entry Application (DEA) Germany

Enter the details such as

  • Mode of transport (By air or sea or land)
  • travel details such as flight number, flight name etc
  • Personal details such as name, contact number etc
  • Verification
  • Address
  • Number of people travelling with you
  • Summary

PCR Test location in Germany

COVID-19 test centers are located in Frankfurt, Munich and many other German airports.
