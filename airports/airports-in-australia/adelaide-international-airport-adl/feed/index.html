Adelaide’s original Commonwealth Government aerodrome, purchased from Captain Harry Butler, was developed in 1921 on just 24 hectares of land at Hendon (the site of the former Philips factory and SA Film Corporation). Nine kilometres from the city, the aerodrome provided facilities for airmail services between Adelaide and Sydney. By 1927 Adelaide’s aviation needs had grown considerably. The site of today’s Para field Airport was acquired and progressively developed. By 1941 the State’s aviation needs had outgrown Para field. The West Torrens site, also known as West Beach, was chosen for Adelaide Airport.
